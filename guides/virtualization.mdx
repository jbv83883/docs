<Note>
The exact steps to enter BIOS and enable virtualization might vary slightly depending on your computer's manufacturer. Refer to your motherboard or computer manual for specific instructions.
</Note>

## Step 1: Access BIOS

1. **Restart Your Computer**
2. **Enter BIOS**
    - During the boot process, press the BIOS key. Common keys include `F2`, `F10`, `Delete`, or `Esc`. The key may be displayed on the screen during startup.

## Step 2: Enable Virtualization

1. **Navigate to the CPU Configuration Section**
    - Use the arrow keys to navigate to `Advanced`, `Advanced Mode`, `Configuration`, or a similar section where CPU settings are found.

2. **Find the Virtualization Setting**
    - Look for options labeled `Intel Virtualization Technology`, `VT-x`, `AMD-V`, `SVM Mode`, or similar.

3. **Enable Virtualization**
    - Select the virtualization option and change it to `Enabled`.

4. **Save and Exit**
    - Press `F10` to save changes and exit BIOS. Confirm if prompted.
